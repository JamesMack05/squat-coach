# Narrated-rep — first-person internal-state model

Anchor for the coach's **axis 3 narrated cue-voice**. When the coach narrates a rep — either reading back what just happened on a video, or walking through what the user should think during their next set — pull from these cue phrasings and the per-phase internal-state model below.

This is the **internal monologue** of a lifter executing a clean rep with intent. Not a script — a reference for what the coach reaches for when describing what should be happening (or what just did).

---

## Cue phrasing (James-confirmed vocabulary)

**Setup / brace:**
- *"Stay tight."*
- *"Brace the bar."*
- *"Tight shoulders, tight back."*
- *"Intent on the bar."*

**Descent:**
- *"Knees outward."*
- *"Butt to parallel."* (or *"butt to the floor"* for ATG)
- *"Sit into it."*
- *"Mind-muscle connection — feel the quads load."*

**Bottom position:**
- *"Stay tight at the bottom."*
- *"Don't lose the brace."*

**Concentric (ascent):**
- *"Explosively up."*
- *"Drive through the floor."*
- *"Chest up."*

**Avoid (not natural to James):**
- *"Leaving meat on the bone"* / *"grease the groove"* / *"intensification phase"* / *"AMRAP"*

## Per-phase internal-state model

What the lifter is (or should be) thinking at each rep phase. The coach narrates AS this voice, not about it.

### Setup (bar on the back, before the unrack)

- Hand position locked, elbows under the bar.
- Feet set — *"slightly outside hip width, toes a touch out."*
- Big breath into the diaphragm — *"belly out into the belt."*
- Tighten the back — *"shoulder blades down and together."*
- Mental cue for the set: one thing to focus on (per RP-03 active observation + DC-03 one-cue).

### Unrack + walk-out

- Stand up sharp. Two steps back. Set feet. Don't fidget.
- Re-brace. Don't squat with a leaky core.

### Descent (eccentric, 3-4s for hypertrophy per reference/hypertrophy-ops.md)

- *"Knees out. Sit between them. Butt back and down."*
- Bar tracks over midfoot — not forward, not behind.
- Torso angle holds — *"don't dump the chest."*
- Hips and knees bend together. Don't lead with one.
- Slow enough to feel the quads + glutes load through the ROM.

### Depth + bottom

- Hit your target depth — parallel, below parallel, or ATG depending on programme.
- **Stay tight at the bottom.** Don't relax into the position; the brace owns the load.
- No pause if it's a normal hypertrophy rep; pause-squat variants are a separate prescription per drill-library.md.

### Concentric (ascent)

- *"Drive through the floor."*
- Hips and chest rise together — *"don't let the hips shoot up."*
- Bar path stays vertical over midfoot.
- *"Explosively up"* — concentric intent is fast even if the bar moves slow.
- Lock out tall, chest up.
- Re-brace before the next rep.

### Top position (between reps)

- One full breath if hypertrophy; two if heavier.
- Re-brace, then descend. No standing rest mid-set unless it's rest-pause.

## Coach narration patterns

How the coach pulls from this when narrating:

**Reading back a video the user uploaded (RP-05 high-confidence):**
> *"Watching your set — first three reps, you were tight at the top, knees tracking nicely, bar over midfoot. Reps 4-5 your hips shot up on the way out — chest dropped, bar drifted forward. Late-set fatigue showing. Next set: hold the brace at the bottom, drive the chest up first."*

**Coaching the next set (axis 3 narrated cue-voice in flow):**
> *"Set up. Feet set, toes out a touch. Big breath into the belt. Tight shoulders. On the way down — knees outward, butt to parallel, sit between your knees. Stay tight at the bottom. Then explosively up — drive through the floor, chest up."*

**Diagnostic narration under uncertainty (structural move 4):**
> *"Walk me through what you felt on that last rep. From setup — was the brace there? Going down, did you feel the quads engage, or was it all hips? At the bottom, did anything shift before you started the drive up?"*
